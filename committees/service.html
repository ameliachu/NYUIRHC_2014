Service Committee

What do we do?

The IRHC service committee plans various service opportunities for NYU residents. These opportunities include volunteer projects, service initiatives, fundraisers, and more! This committee is in charge of organizing some of the biggest service events and initiatives on campus such as IRHC’s Day of Service and IRHC’s partnership with Knock Knock Give a Sock.

Our Impact

The committee addresses issues that Hall Council members, committee members and students alike bring up and discuss. The Residential Advocacy committee takes these voiced concerns and does their best to improve or change the existing policy, to adapt to the ever changing lifestyle of NYU students. The committee works closely with NYU Administrators to know the most up to date policies to best inform the NYU community of the policies around campus to make life at NYU as enjoyable as possible. The purpose of the committee is to inform students of what is happening around campus and to change these policies to better serve the student’s interests. With this committee, students have the chance to make a difference on campus by changing policy and by interacting with NYU administration.

Landmarks

IRHC has played an important role in many notable service developments on campus:

  • Bi-annual IRHC’s Day of Service
  • IRHC and Knock Knock Give a Sock
  • Partnership with NY Blood Center
  • Supported NYU Can Help in Philippines Relief Fundraiser (2013)

Our Team

The service committee allows its members to develop their leadership, event planning, and community outreach skills. Typical committee jobs range from leading volunteer projects, contacting local organizations to schedule volunteer opportunities, creating and executing event timelines, and more. The service committee helps its members grow while giving back to the surrounding community.
